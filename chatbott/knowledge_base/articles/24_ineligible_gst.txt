INELIGIBLE GST (BLOCKED ITC)

Purpose: Handle blocked Input Tax Credit (ITC) scenarios where GST cannot be recovered under Section 17(5) of CGST Act.

WHAT IS INELIGIBLE GST:
- GST paid on purchases/expenses that cannot be claimed as ITC under GST law
- Portion of GST that cannot be recovered from government
- Must be treated as cost to business

USE CASES (As per Section 17(5) of CGST Act):

Blocked ITC Scenarios:
- Motor vehicles for personal use
- Food, beverages, outdoor catering
- Travel benefits to employees
- Membership of clubs, health, fitness centers
- Personal expenses
- Goods/services for personal consumption
- Works contract for immovable property
- Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off
- Goods given as free samples

SYSTEM FUNCTIONALITY:
- Posts GST amount back to expense account instead of GST input account
- Increases the cost of the expense
- No ITC claim available

CONFIGURATION STEPS:

1. Create Vendor Bill:
   - Go to: Transactions > Payables > Enter Bills
   - Fill all required header details

2. Expense Line Entry:
   - Select Account (e.g., "6400 Insurance - Property & General Liability")
   - Select HSN/SAC Code (e.g., 996812)
   - Enter Amount (e.g., 100.00)
   - Select Tax Code (e.g., "CGST:CGST/SGST @18%")

3. Apply Ineligible GST:
   - Scroll right on the expense line
   - Locate "Ineligible GST Type" column
   - Select from dropdown:
     * Blocked ITC 17(5): As per CGST Act Section 17(5)
     * Ineligible ITC (POS Rule): Place of Supply rule

4. Click Save

GL IMPACT VERIFICATION:

Go to "GL Impact" subtab

Standard GL Impact (without Ineligible GST):
- Expense Account: Debit 100.00
- Input CGST: Debit 9.00
- Input SGST: Debit 9.00
- Accounts Payable: Credit 118.00

Ineligible GST Impact:
- Expense Account: Debit 100.00 (original)
- 2190 Tax Liability: Debit 7.00 (liability adjustment)
- Input CGST: Debit 9.00 (normal entry)
- Input SGST: Debit 9.00 (normal entry)
- Input SGST: Credit 9.00 (reversal)
- Input CGST: Credit 9.00 (reversal)
- Expense Account: Debit 18.00 (GST added to cost)

Net Effect: Expense = 118.00 (100 + 18 GST)

REPORTING IMPACT:

Ineligible GST reflects in GSTR-3B report:
- Shows under ineligible ITC sections
- Properly categorized for compliance
- Table 4(D) - ITC Reversed

INELIGIBLE ITC TYPES:

Blocked ITC 17(5):
- As per Section 17(5) of CGST Act
- Specific categories blocked
- Full GST non-recoverable

Ineligible ITC (POS Rule):
- Place of Supply rules
- Location-based restrictions
- Partial or full blocking

COMMON SCENARIOS:

Personal Vehicle Maintenance:
- Service: Vehicle repairs
- GST: Non-recoverable
- Treatment: Add to expense

Employee Food & Beverages:
- Service: Catering, restaurants
- GST: Non-recoverable (with exceptions)
- Treatment: Add to expense

Club Membership:
- Service: Health/fitness clubs
- GST: Non-recoverable
- Treatment: Add to expense

Personal Insurance:
- Service: Life insurance
- GST: Non-recoverable
- Treatment: Add to expense

KEY FEATURES:
- Automatic GL reversal entries
- Increases expense by GST amount
- Compliance with ITC blocking provisions
- Proper reporting in GSTR-3B

BEST PRACTICES:
- Identify blocked ITC scenarios
- Configure at time of bill entry
- Review GL impact before posting
- Maintain documentation
- Regular compliance review
- Train AP team on scenarios

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Ineligible GST setting affects ITC claiming
- System automatically reverses input credit
- Expense increases by GST amount
- Critical for accurate GSTR-3B reporting
- Cannot claim ITC once marked ineligible
- Proper classification essential for compliance
