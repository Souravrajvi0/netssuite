ACCOUNT MASTER GST SETUP

Purpose: Set up GL accounts with GST parameters.

Navigation Path: List > Accounting > Accounts > New

GST FIELDS AT BODY LEVEL:

1. Nature of Item: Classification (Goods/Services)
2. HSN/SAC Code: Applicable code
3. Ineligible GST: For blocked ITC scenarios
4. RCM Applicable: Reverse Charge Mechanism flag

USAGE: 
These details auto-populate when the account is used in transactions.

ACCOUNT TYPES AND GST CONFIGURATION:

Expense Accounts:
- Used on vendor bills
- Configure for common expenses
- RCM flag for applicable scenarios
- Ineligible GST for blocked ITC

Income Accounts:
- Used on customer invoices
- HSN/SAC for revenue classification
- Nature of item configuration

NATURE OF ITEM:

Goods:
- Tangible products
- Physical inventory
- Asset purchases

Services:
- Intangible offerings
- Professional services
- Consulting, support

INELIGIBLE GST SCENARIOS:

As per Section 17(5) of CGST Act, ITC blocked for:
- Motor vehicles (personal use)
- Food and beverages
- Outdoor catering
- Travel benefits
- Membership fees (clubs, health centers)
- Personal expenses

When Ineligible GST is checked:
- GST amount added to expense
- No ITC claim available
- Shows in GSTR-3B ineligible section

RCM APPLICABLE:

Reverse Charge Mechanism scenarios:
- Services from unregistered vendors
- Legal services
- Director fees
- Import of services
- GTA services
- Security services

When RCM is checked:
- Special tax treatment applied
- Buyer liable to pay tax
- No tax collected from vendor

CONFIGURATION STEPS:

1. Open Account Record:
   - Navigate to: List > Accounting > Accounts
   - Select existing or create new

2. Configure GST Fields:
   - Nature of Item: Select appropriate type
   - HSN/SAC Code: Enter if applicable
   - Ineligible GST: Check if ITC blocked
   - RCM Applicable: Check if reverse charge applies

3. Save Account:
   - Click Save
   - Test on sample transaction

COMMON ACCOUNT CONFIGURATIONS:

Travel Expense Account:
├── Nature: Services
├── HSN/SAC: 996411
├── Ineligible GST: Checked (if personal travel)
└── RCM Applicable: Unchecked

Professional Fees Account:
├── Nature: Services
├── HSN/SAC: 998313
├── Ineligible GST: Unchecked
└── RCM Applicable: May be checked (if from unregistered)

Rent Expense Account:
├── Nature: Services
├── HSN/SAC: 997212
├── Ineligible GST: Unchecked
└── RCM Applicable: May be checked

BENEFITS OF ACCOUNT-LEVEL GST SETUP:

1. Automation: GST details pre-fill on bills
2. Compliance: Ensures proper ITC treatment
3. Accuracy: Correct RCM application
4. Speed: Faster transaction processing
5. Reporting: Accurate GSTR-2 and GSTR-3B

EXAMPLE SCENARIOS:

Scenario 1 - Professional Services (Registered Vendor):
- Account: Professional Fees
- Nature: Services
- SAC: 998313
- Ineligible GST: No
- RCM: No
- Result: Normal GST, ITC available

Scenario 2 - Professional Services (Unregistered Vendor):
- Account: Professional Fees
- Nature: Services
- SAC: 998313
- Ineligible GST: No
- RCM: Yes
- Result: RCM applied, buyer pays tax

Scenario 3 - Personal Vehicle Expense:
- Account: Vehicle Maintenance
- Nature: Services
- SAC: 998719
- Ineligible GST: Yes
- RCM: No
- Result: GST added to expense, no ITC

BEST PRACTICES:

- Configure GST fields on commonly used accounts
- Review account setup periodically
- Update when GST rules change
- Document account GST configurations
- Train AP/AR teams on proper account selection
- Test accounts with sample transactions
- Maintain list of RCM applicable accounts

IMPORTANT NOTES:

- Account-level setup is optional but recommended
- Can override on individual transactions if needed
- Ineligible GST setting affects ITC claiming
- RCM flag triggers special tax calculation
- Proper setup ensures compliance
- Critical for accurate GSTR-2 and GSTR-3B reporting
- Changes don't affect past transactions
